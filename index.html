AI for Computational Biology, Life Science, and Molecular Discovery

About

LSAIL studies computational biology at the interface of life science and artificial intelligence. We develop AI methods that connect molecular structures with biological context, including phenotype, genotype, transcriptomics, morphology, and other multimodal life science data.

Our work spans broad computational biology, with major applications in drug discovery, toxicity and safety prediction, and biology-aware molecular modeling.

Key Topics

Computational Biology

AI methods for molecular, cellular, and multi-omics data.

Drug Discovery

Virtual screening, candidate prioritization, and molecular design.

Toxicity & Safety

hERG, hepatotoxicity, genotoxicity, and broader ADMET-related tasks.

Biology-aware Representation

Molecular representations enriched with phenotype, genotype, transcriptomics, and morphology.

Genotype/Phenotype-guided Modeling

AI models that connect biological state information with prediction and design.

Multimodal Learning

Integrating chemical and biological information for translational research.

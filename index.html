About the Lab

AI for Drug Discovery & Computational Biology

Biologically Informed AI, from Molecule to Patient

LSAIL builds AI methods that span the drug discovery pipeline — from generative molecular design and virtual screening to cardiac safety and in vivo toxicity prediction.

A central theme of our work is biology-aware learning: enriching molecular representations with genotype, phenotype, transcriptomics, and morphology so that AI models reflect real biological context — not just chemistry.

Recent work includes a genotype-to-drug diffusion model for generating tailored anti-cancer small molecules (Nature Communications, 2025) and robust, interpretable deep learning for hERG channel blocker prediction (Briefings in Bioinformatics, 2022).

We're Looking for Passionate Students

If you're curious about how AI can accelerate drug discovery and change the way we understand biology — we'd love to hear from you.

LSAIL is actively recruiting passionate and curious students — graduate (M.S. / Ph.D.) and undergraduate researchers — who want to work at the frontier of AI, drug discovery, and computational biology.

We don't require a perfect background. We care about curiosity, persistence, and a willingness to learn across fields. Students from computer science, AI, chemistry, biology, pharmacy, statistics, and related areas are all welcome — and so are undergrads who just want to try research for the first time.

What you'll do here:

• Work on real, publishable problems in AI-driven drug discovery and computational biology
• Learn deep learning, cheminformatics, and biology-aware modeling hands-on
• Collaborate across AI, biology, and chemistry — not just sit behind one screen
• Be supported as a researcher, not just a pair of hands
